Types of Classes

Here's a brief description of some of the most popular group fitness classes we offer:

Yoga

Yoga classes are fantastic for both your body and mind. In a basic yoga session, an instructor guides you through various stretches and poses while emphasizing breath control and mindfulness. Common yoga poses include downward-facing dog, upward-facing dog, tree pose, mountain pose, and warrior poses. Whether you're a beginner or more experienced, yoga offers improved muscle tone, flexibility, and stress reduction.

Spin (Indoor Cycling)

Spin classes involve a group of people pedaling stationary bikes together under the guidance of an instructor. These classes start with a warm-up and gradually increase intensity. You'll pedal at different speeds and resistance levels, sometimes even "hovering" over the bike. It's a high-energy cardio workout that gets your heart pumping and legs burning.

Boot Camp

Inspired by military training, boot camp classes are intense and challenging. They combine strength training, cardiovascular exercises, and agility drills. Expect a mix of bodyweight exercises, weights, and functional movements. Boot camp classes are great for those who thrive on high-intensity workouts and want to push their limits.

Hybrid Classes

These classes blend different exercise styles. For example, you might find a class that combines elements of yoga, Pilates, and strength training. Hybrid classes keep things interesting and target various muscle groups.

Barre

Barre classes draw inspiration from ballet, incorporating small, controlled movements using a ballet barre. These workouts focus on sculpting and toning muscles, especially in the legs, core, and arms. No dance experience required-just a desire for a graceful burn.

Pilates

Pilates emphasizes core strength, stability, and flexibility. It involves controlled movements and specific breathing patterns. Pilates classes often use specialized equipment like reformers, but mat-based classes are also popular. Expect improved posture, balance, and overall body awareness.

Orangetheory

Orangetheory Fitness combines cardiovascular intervals, strength training, and endurance exercises. Participants wear heart rate monitors, aiming to spend time in the "orange zone" (high-intensity effort). It's a full-body workout designed to boost metabolism and burn calories.

CrossFit

CrossFit is a high-intensity functional fitness program that incorporates weightlifting, gymnastics, and cardiovascular exercises. Classes are typically done in a group setting, and each day's workout (or "WOD") varies. CrossFit enthusiasts love the community spirit and the challenge of constantly varied workouts.
